# GDPR Checklist (EU General Data Protection Regulation)

Regulation (EU) 2016/679. In force since 2018-05-25. Applies to all processing of EU residents' personal data, regardless of controller's location.

## Mandatory Disclosures in Privacy Notice (Articles 13–14)

Required when collecting data directly from the data subject (Art. 13) and when obtained from other sources (Art. 14).

### Identity and Contact

1. Controller identity — name, address, contact
2. DPO (Data Protection Officer) contact — if appointed
3. EU Representative contact — if controller is outside the EU (Art. 27)

### Processing Details

4. Purposes of the processing
5. Legal basis under Article 6 (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests)
6. If legitimate interests — the specific interests pursued
7. Categories of personal data collected (esp. if indirect collection)
8. Recipients or categories of recipients
9. Transfers to third countries — including adequacy decisions, SCCs, BCRs, safeguards

### Retention

10. Retention period or criteria to determine it

### Rights (8 data subject rights)

11. Right of access (Art. 15)
12. Right to rectification (Art. 16)
13. Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17)
14. Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18)
15. Right to data portability (Art. 20)
16. Right to object (Art. 21)
17. Rights relating to automated decision-making (Art. 22)
18. Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) — where consent is the legal basis

### Complaints and Further Info

19. Right to lodge complaint with a supervisory authority
20. Whether provision of data is statutory/contractual requirement, and consequences of not providing
21. Existence of automated decision-making including profiling — logic, significance, consequences

## Legal Bases (Article 6)

Processing lawful only if one applies:

- **Consent** — freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous (Art. 7)
- **Contract** — necessary for contract performance with data subject
- **Legal obligation** — compliance with law
- **Vital interests** — protecting life
- **Public task** — official authority
- **Legitimate interests** — balancing test required; not for public authorities in their tasks

## Special Categories (Article 9)

Processing of racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic/biometric data, health data, sex life/orientation is **prohibited** unless specific exception (explicit consent, employment law, vital interests, etc.).

## Data Subject Rights Deadlines

Controller must respond within **1 month** of request (extendable by 2 months for complex requests — max 3 months total).

## Data Breach Notification (Articles 33–34)

- Notify supervisory authority within **72 hours** unless unlikely to result in risk
- Notify affected data subjects **without undue delay** if high risk

## Data Transfers to Third Countries (Chapter V)

Permissible mechanisms:
- **Adequacy decision** (UK, Switzerland, Japan, South Korea, etc.)
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)** — EU Commission approved
- **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)**
- **Derogations** (Art. 49) — limited cases

## Penalties (Article 83)

- Tier 1: up to **€10 million or 2% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher
- Tier 2 (severe): up to **€20 million or 4% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher

## Cookies and ePrivacy

GDPR + **ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC** (member state implementations, e.g., UK PECR).

- Cookies other than strictly necessary require **prior opt-in consent**
- Consent must be **specific per purpose** (analytics, advertising, etc.)
- Pre-ticked boxes are NOT valid consent (CJEU Planet49 ruling)
- "Reject all" must be as prominent as "Accept all" (CNIL/EDPB guidance)

## Children (Article 8)

For "information society services" offered directly to children:
- Under **16** (member states may lower to 13)
- Parental consent required

## Key Differences vs Korea PIPA

| Aspect | GDPR (EU) | PIPA (KR) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Consent model | Opt-in, granular | Opt-in, with exceptions |
| Data subject rights | 8 explicit rights | Similar with differences |
| Breach notification | 72 hours to authority | Without delay |
| DPO requirement | Mandatory for certain controllers | CPO mandatory for most |
| Max penalty | 4% global turnover | 10% domestic revenue (2026.9) |
| Child age | 16 (or 13 per state) | 14 |
| Cookie consent | Explicit opt-in | Recommended, not yet mandated strictly |
| Extraterritorial | Yes (controllers targeting EU) | Partial (domestic agent rule) |

## When to Use This Checklist

Use `jurisdictions/eu-gdpr/` when:
- Service targets EU residents (regardless of controller's location)
- Offering goods/services to EU users
- Monitoring behavior of EU users
- Having EU establishment

Combine with Korean checklist when service operates in both regions (most common for Korean global services).
